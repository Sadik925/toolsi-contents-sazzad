Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a UUID?

A UUID is a 128-bit identifier used to uniquely tag objects, records, or information across distributed systems or databases.

What version of UUID does this tool generate?

This tool generates UUID version 4, which is randomly generated and widely used in modern applications.

Is each UUID truly unique?

Yes, UUID v4 is designed to be statistically unique. The probability of duplication is extremely low due to its large keyspace.

Can I use this UUID in production environments?

Absolutely. The generated UUIDs are suitable for production use in APIs, database keys, or any system requiring unique identifiers.
