Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What does URL decoding mean?

URL decoding is the process of converting percent-encoded characters (like %20) in a URL back to their original form, such as spaces or special symbols.

Why do URLs get encoded?

URLs are encoded to ensure safe transmission over the internet. Special characters like spaces or symbols are replaced with percent signs and hexadecimal codes.

Is it safe to use an online URL decoder?

Yes, it’s safe as long as the tool doesn’t store or transmit your data. Our tool processes everything in your browser for complete privacy.

Can I decode full links or just query parameters?

You can decode the entire URL, including the domain, path, and query parameters. The tool will convert all encoded parts of the input string.
